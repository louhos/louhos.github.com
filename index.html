With open data on governance, economics, traffic, geographic information rapidly increasing, there is a clear need for free and open tools for analyzing it. A variety of public and private organizations, such as the National Land Survey of Finland and Statistics Finland, are taking steps towards better access to publicly funded data. Making the analytical tools widely available and facilitating their use will increase the value of the public data resources.

Here at Louhos we aim at:

  1. Providing a central hub that facilitates efficient access and utilization of public data resources
  2. Developing analytical tools and using them for new insights into how the society works
  3. Actually, we think Simon Rogers said it better we ever could, so read the quote on the right

If you asked me what a data scientist was, I would say someone who can bridge the raw data and the analysis - and make it accessible. It's a democratising role; by bringing the data to the people, you make the world just a little bit better

Simon Rogers, Guardian.

Projects

Louhos is a community-driven collection of open source code and tools for open data in Finland. The Louhos repository is hosted at github. Contributions to facilitate the downloading, preprocessing, analysis, and visualization, written in any language, are welcome.

One of the projects hosted by Louhos is the comprehensive R library for Open Data in Finland, coined soRvi (Eng. 'turning machine'), which provides programmatic access to hundreds of Finnish data resources. The R environment enables seamless integration of open data streams from various sources with professional data analysis and visualization tools. The toolkit received a double award in Private Data opening category in the national Apps4Finland 2011 competition (press release).

Community

Louhos is a community-driven project all the way. We welcome feedback and material from new contributors. While our focus is on Finnish open data and tools, we call the open data community to launch a network of similar initiatives, suited for local standards and needs and to share the efforts, code and fun!
