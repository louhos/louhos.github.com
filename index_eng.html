Open Data in Finland

The public availability of societal data on governance, economics, traffic, geographic information is rapidly increasing in Finland.

A variety of public and private organizations, such as the National Land Survey of Finland and Statistics Finland, are taking remarkable steps towards better access to publicly funded data. Even more remarkably, the movement is largely driven by grass-roots activity of individual citizens.

Purpose

With all the data becoming available, there is a clear need for free and open analytical tools. Making such tools widely available and facilitating their use will increase the value of the public data resources

Until now, the lack of suitable tools has formed a bottleneck for the availability of 'open' data as efficient access often requires technical expertise regarding a wide variety of tools and data formats.

Community

What really facilitates the adoption of public data resources is a coordinated and community-driven action.

We welcome feedback and material from new contributors. Our focus is on Finnish open data, but we call the open data community to launch a network of similar initiatives, suited for local standards and needs and to share the efforts and code.

Let's stay connected!

Projects

Louhos is a community-driven collection of open source code and tools for open data in Finland. Louhos forms a central hub that facilitates efficient public access and utilization to open data streams. The Louhos repository is hosted at github. Contributions to facilitate the downloading, preprocessing, analysis, and visualization, written in any language, are welcome.

One of the projects hosted by Louhos is the comprehensive R library for Open Data in Finland, coined soRvi (Eng. 'turning machine'), which provides programmatic access to hundreds of Finnish data resources. The R environment enables seamless integration of open data streams from various sources to professional data analysis and visualization tools. The toolkit received a double award in Private Data opening category in the national Apps4Finland 2011 competition (press release).
