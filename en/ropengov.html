--- layout: page root: true title: tagline: rOpenGov version: 0.3 ghurl: https://github.com/louhos/louhos.github.com language: en group: project translation: index.html --- {% include JB/setup %} Algorithms for Open Government Data and Computational Social Science

We have seen an avalanche of open data on governance, economics, demography and other fields relevant to computational social science. Dedicated analytical tools are urgently needed to take full advantage of these new information resources, including tools to access, clean up and integrate data on government and society as well as new algorithms to better understand and communicate such information in a fully transparent and reproducible manner.

A versatile community-driven ecosystem of analytical software packages dedicated to social and political sciences can provide a solution. Supporting a community of independent developers could revolutionize the field as has already happened for biological sciences during the last decade, and build on lessons learned from analogous projects in computational biology, such as Bioconductor.

For coherence, we are calling for contributions in the form of R packages. We collect computational social science software from independent developers within a single umbrella project. If you are writing related software on other languages than R, we would love to hear about your project. We need your support - don't hesitate to contact us to give feedback or suggestions!

Projects:

  • Austria Grazwahl2012 R tools for Austria elections
  • Finland sorvi R package for open government data in Finland
  • Russia R tools for open data in Russia - coming soon
  • Further projects coming, submissions welcome!
