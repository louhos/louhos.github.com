--- layout: page root: true title: tagline: rOpenGov version: 0.3 ghurl: https://github.com/louhos/louhos.github.com language: en group: project translation: index.html --- {% include JB/setup %} Algorithms for Open Government Data and Computational Social Science

In the recent years we have seen an avalanche of open data on governance, economics, demography and other fields relevant to computational social science.

Efficient analytical tools are urgently needed to take full advantage of these new information resources, including dedicated tools to access, clean up and integrate data on government and society as well as new algorithms to better understand and communicate complex societal data streams in a fully transparent and reproducible manner.

Hence, we suggest the creation of a versatile community-driven ecosystem of analytical software packages dedicated to social sciences. To advance this goal, we have started to collect computational software dedicated for social and political sciences.

For coherence, we have decided to focus on R packages. Supporting a community of independent developers could revolutionize the field as has already happened for biological sciences during the last decade, and build on lessons learned from analogous projects in computational biology, such as Bioconductor or QIIME. However, if you are writing related software on other languages, we would love to hear about your project.

Contribute to the movement! We need your support - don't hesitate to contact us to give feedback or suggestions!

  • Finland sorvi R package for open government data in Finland
