Motivation With open data on governance, economics, demography and other fields relevant to computational social science rapidly increasing, there is a need for new analytical tools to explore this emerging treasure trove for quantitative research. A versatile and decentralized, yet coordinated ecosystem of compatible analytical software packages dedicated to social sciences could revolutionize the field as already happened for biological sciences during the last decade.

Solution We propose creating a decentralized analytical software package ecosystem for computational languages that is dedicated for social and political sciences and supports transparent and reproducible analytical workflows from raw data to analysis, statistical testing and visualization. Decentralization will be essential to scale up the efforts and to adjust the tools for local needs that may vary across geographical regions and application problems. Coordination of the efforts will, however, help to pool resources, building on lessons learned from analogous, wildly successful projects from computational biology such as Bioconductor and QIIME.

Next steps We welcome contributions in the form of software packages in R, Python or other analytical languages dedicated to utilization of public data resources in computational social science. A dedicated website for the project will be opened soon. Meanwhile, don't hesitate to contact us.
