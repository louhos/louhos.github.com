Motivation With open data on governance, economics, demography and other fields relevant to computational social science rapidly increasing, there is a need for analytical tools to explore this emerging treasure trove for quantitative research. A versatile and decentralized, yet coordinated ecosystem of analytical software packages dedicated to social sciences could revolutionize the field as already happened for biological sciences during the last decade.

Solution We propose creating an ecosystem of computational software for social and political sciences that supports reproducible analytical workflows from raw data to analysis, statistical testing and visualization. The efforts need to be decentralized to scale up the efforts and to adjust the tools for local needs that may vary across geographical regions and application domains. Coordination will, however, help to pool resources and avoid reinventing the wheel. We build on lessons learned from analogous, wildly successful projects in computational biology, including as Bioconductor and QIIME.
